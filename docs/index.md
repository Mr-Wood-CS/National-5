# Welcome to National 5 Computing Science

National 5 Computing Science helps you develop knowledge and understanding of computing science. You will learn how computer systems work, how data is represented, and how digital solutions are designed, created, tested and evaluated.

The course also develops computational thinking and practical problem-solving skills. These skills are used to solve problems in software, databases and websites.

## Course Content

The course has four areas of study:

- Software Design and Development
- Computer Systems
- Database Design and Development
- Web Design and Development

However, the course assessment is not the same for every learner. Everyone studies the mandatory areas, then completes one optional area.

!!! note "Important"

    **Software Design and Development** and **Computer Systems** are mandatory.

    You will then study **either Database Design and Development or Web Design and Development**.

    Your optional area is assessed in both the question paper and the practical assignment.

## Mandatory Areas

### Software Design and Development

In this area, you will develop programming and computational-thinking skills. You will learn how to analyse problems, design solutions, implement programs, test them and evaluate how well they work.

You will learn about:

- Development methodologies
- Analysis, design, implementation, testing and evaluation
- Programming using a high-level language
- Data types and structures
- Selection and repetition
- Standard algorithms
- Testing with normal, extreme and exceptional data
- Evaluating software solutions

### Computer Systems

In this area, you will learn how computer systems store and process data. You will also learn about basic computer architecture, environmental impact and security precautions.

You will learn about:

- Binary representation of positive integers
- Floating point representation
- Extended ASCII
- Bitmap and vector graphics
- Basic computer architecture
- Translators, including interpreters and compilers
- Environmental impact of computer systems
- Firewalls and encryption

## Optional Area

You will study one of the following optional areas.

### Database Design and Development

In this area, you will develop knowledge, understanding and practical problem-solving skills in database design and development.

You will learn about:

- Analysis of database problems
- End-user and functional requirements
- UK GDPR
- Entity-relationship diagrams
- Data dictionaries
- Primary keys and foreign keys
- Relational databases with two linked tables
- SQL operations including SELECT, INSERT, UPDATE and DELETE
- Testing and evaluating database solutions

### Web Design and Development

In this area, you will develop knowledge, understanding and practical problem-solving skills in web design and development.

You will learn about:

- Analysis of website problems
- End-user and functional requirements
- Website structure
- User-interface design and wireframes
- Copyright law relating to web content
- File formats and compression
- HTML
- CSS
- JavaScript mouse events
- Testing and evaluating web-based solutions

## Question Paper

The question paper is worth 80 marks and lasts 1 hour and 30 minutes.

The question paper has three sections. Section 1 is mandatory. You then complete either Section 2 or Section 3.

| Question Paper Section | Area Assessed | Marks |
|------------------------|---------------|------:|
| Section 1 | Software Design and Development, and Computer Systems | 55 |
| Section 2 | Database Design and Development | 25 |
| Section 3 | Web Design and Development | 25 |

!!! note "Question paper choice"

    Everyone completes **Section 1**.

    You then complete **either Section 2 or Section 3**, depending on whether you are studying Database Design and Development or Web Design and Development.

## Practical Assignment

The practical assignment is worth 40 marks and is completed under supervised open-book conditions.

The assignment has three tasks. Task 1 is mandatory. You then complete either Task 2 or Task 3.

Computer Systems is **not** assessed in the practical assignment.

| Assignment Task | Area Assessed | Marks |
|-----------------|---------------|------:|
| Task 1 | Software Design and Development | 25 |
| Task 2 | Database Design and Development | 15 |
| Task 3 | Web Design and Development | 15 |

!!! note "Assignment choice"

    Everyone completes **Task 1**.

    You then complete **either Task 2 or Task 3**, depending on whether you are studying Database Design and Development or Web Design and Development.

## Overall Course Assessment

Your final grade is based on the total marks achieved across the question paper and the practical assignment.

| Assessment Component | Marks | Percentage |
|---------------------|------:|-----------:|
| Question Paper | 80 | 67% |
| Practical Assignment | 40 | 33% |
| **Total** | **120** | **100%** |

## Skills Developed

By studying National 5 Computing Science, you will develop skills in:

- Computational thinking
- Problem solving
- Analysing problems
- Designing digital solutions
- Implementing solutions
- Testing and evaluation
- Communicating computing concepts using correct terminology
- Understanding the impact of computing technologies

Successful completion of National 5 Computing Science can lead to:

- Higher Computing Science
- Other qualifications in Computing Science or related areas
- Further study, employment or training
