# Design

Design is the planning stage of software development. Before a programmer writes any code, they should think carefully about how the solution will work, what data will be needed and how the user will interact with the program.

## What is Design?

Design is the process of creating a plan for a solution before implementation begins. Just as an architect creates plans before constructing a building, programmers create designs before writing code.

Design techniques help programmers organise their ideas, communicate solutions clearly and ensure that all requirements have been considered before development starts.

Efficient problem-solving in computing begins with careful planning and the use of appropriate design techniques to ensure solutions are both effective and easy to implement.

Before a programmer begins writing code, they should create a design for their solution. A good design acts as a blueprint, helping to identify potential problems early, organise ideas clearly and reduce mistakes during implementation. Spending time on design often saves time later in the development process.

At National 5 level, there are four key design techniques you need to understand:

* **Structure diagrams** provide a visual breakdown of a solution, showing how a large problem can be divided into smaller, more manageable tasks.

* **Flowcharts** illustrate the logical flow of processes, making it easier to follow the sequence of steps, decisions and actions within an algorithm.
  
* **Pseudocode** bridges the gap between natural language and programming by offering a simplified, language-independent way to represent a solution before it is coded.

* **Wireframes** are simple sketches or digital drafts that show the layout of a user interface. They help designers plan where input controls, buttons, menus, images and output areas will be placed before development begins.

## Why is Design Important?

Good design helps programmers:

* plan solutions before writing code
* identify errors and missing requirements early
* communicate ideas with other developers and stakeholders
* create programs that are easier to test, maintain and improve
