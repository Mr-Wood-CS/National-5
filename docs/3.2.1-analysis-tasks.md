# Analysis Tasks

## Task One

A used-car dealership has six showrooms in different locations across Scotland. It wants to create a relational database to store details of cars owned by the company and details of each of their showrooms.

The database will allow sales staff to view details of specific cars and to see which showroom the cars are located in.

The developers have asked some of the sales staff what they would like to see in the database.

Below are some of the comments they made.

* I need to know details of all the cars we have of a particular make and model.
* I need to be able to find all the cars we have in a particular price range.
* I need to know the colour of a particular car.
* I need to display car details in order of mileage, lowest first. If the mileage of the cars are the same, customers always want to know which car is the cheapest.
* I need to display car details in order of price, cheapest first.
* If I have a customer who wants a car in another showroom, I need to know the address and phone number of the other showrooms.
* I need to know whether the car is diesel, petrol or electric.

Using analysis techniques, identify what is required in terms of end-user and functional requirements.

1. What are the end-user requirements?

2. What are the functional requirements? In your answer, identify suitable entities, attributes, primary keys and any foreign keys required.
